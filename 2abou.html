Places You Can Visit

SIGIRIYA

Sigiriya, also known as Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress and palace ruin located in central Sri Lanka.
Built by King Kashyapa in the 5th century, it's renowned for its remarkably preserved frescoes, sophisticated water gardens, and the iconic Lion Gate.
The site is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a major tourist attraction.

Why it's worth visiting?
Sigiriya is definitely worth visiting. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site and a stunning ancient rock fortress, offering breathtaking views and a unique historical experience.

KANDY

Kandy, the second largest city in Sri Lanka, is a vibrant cultural and historical hub nestled amidst the scenic hills of the Central Province. It's renowned for the Temple of the Tooth Relic, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major Buddhist pilgrimage destination.

Why it's worth visiting?
Kandy is surrounded by hills, forests and tea plantations. One of the best places to visit in Kandy is the Bahirawakanda Buddha Statue, which is perched high on top of a hill.

NUWARA ELIYA

Nuwara Eliya, often referred to as "Little England", is known for its cool climate, lush tea plantations, and colonial-era architecture.

Why it's worth visiting?
This region is the heart of Sri Lanka's tea industry. Even if you're not a tea drinker, visiting a tea plantation is still worth it.
