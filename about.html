SIGIRIYA

Sigiriya, also known as Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress and palace ruin located in central Sri Lanka.
Built by King Kashyapa in the 5th century, it's renowned for its remarkably preserved frescoes, sophisticated water gardens, and the iconic Lion Gate. The site is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a major tourist attraction.

Why its worth visiting?
Sigiriya is definitely worth visiting.
It's a UNESCO World Heritage site and a stunning ancient rock fortress, offering breathtaking views and a unique historical experience.
While the climb can be challenging, the ancient palace ruins, frescoes, and the overall experience make it a must-see in Sri Lanka.

KANDY

Kandy, the second largest city in Sri Lanka, is a vibrant cultural and historical hub nestled amidst the scenic hills of the Central Province.
It's renowned for the Temple of the Tooth Relic, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major Buddhist pilgrimage destination.
The city is also known for its beautiful Kandy Lake, surrounding tea plantations, and the annual Kandy Esala Perahera festival.

Why its worth visiting?
Kandy is surrounded by hills, forests and tea plantations.
While it is true that these are things Ella and Nuwara Eliya are more famous for, the ones surrounding Kandy are beautiful too. One of the best places to visit in Kandy is the Bahirawakandra Buddha Statue, which is perched high on top of a hill.

NUWARA ELIYA

Nuwara Eliya, often referred to as "Little England", is a city in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, known for its cool climate, lush tea plantations, and colonial-era architecture.
It's a popular hill station and a major tea-growing region, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and historical charm.
